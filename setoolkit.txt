AIM: Assessment of Social Engineering Attacks Using a Phishing Simulation Tool
Learning Objective:
● To understand the concept of social engineering and its significance in cybersecurity.
● To become familiar with the techniques used in phishing attacks, including email
spoofing, deceptive content, and manipulation of human psychology.
● To learn how to use a phishing simulation tool to conduct controlled phishing
campaigns.
● To analyze the results of the phishing simulation, including success rates, common
vulnerabilities, and user behaviors.
● To identify effective strategies for mitigating the risks associated with social engineering
attacks and improving overall cybersecurity posture.
Theory:
● Introduction to Social Engineering: Explanation of social engineering and its various forms,
including phishing, pretexting, and baiting. Discussion on the impact of social engineering
attacks on individuals and organizations.
● Overview of Phishing: Definition of phishing and its prevalence in cyber threats. Examination
of common phishing techniques and tactics.
● Introduction to Phishing Simulation Tool: Demonstration of a real-life phishing simulation
tool, its features, and functionalities. Instructions on how to set up and customize phishing
campaigns using the tool.
● Conducting Phishing Campaigns: Students will be divided into groups and assigned roles
(attackers and defenders). Each group will design and execute a phishing campaign using the
simulation tool. Students will craft phishing emails, choose target recipients, and monitor
campaign progress.
● Analysis of Results: Evaluation of the success rates of phishing campaigns, including the
number of clicks and successful data breaches. Identification of common vulnerabilities
exploited by attackers and weaknesses in organizational defenses.
● Discussion and Reflection: Review of the outcomes of the phishing simulation exercise.
Analysis of user behaviors and responses to phishing attempts. Reflection on the lessons
learned and strategies for improving cybersecurity awareness and resilience.
● Best Practices and Mitigation Strategies: Presentation of best practices for detecting and
mitigating social engineering attacks. Recommendations for implementing security measures
to protect against phishing and other social engineering tactics.
